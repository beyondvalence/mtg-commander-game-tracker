# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MTG Commander players (casual to serious) who track games with their regular playgroup ("pod"). They open the app after a game to log results, or mid-session to check standings. Primary context: around a table with friends, mobile or laptop, relaxed but engaged.

## Product Purpose

Track Commander game results, win rates, commander performance, and player stats across a persistent pod. Success means a player can answer "who's been running away with wins this month?" in two taps.

## Brand Personality

Clean, focused, precise. The app is a sharp tool for people who take their casual game seriously. Voice is confident and direct — no fluff, no hype. The electric green accent is deliberate: it signals the competitive edge without being loud.

## Anti-references

- Generic sports trackers (ESPN-style): loud, bro-ish, broadcast aesthetic, overly large hero stats
- Boring spreadsheet / CRUD enterprise SaaS: gray, table-first, no visual identity
- Cute/cozy card game aesthetic: pastel fantasy, soft and rounded everywhere, Pokemon-adjacent
- Dark navy/purple dev-tool clones: indistinguishable from Linear/Vercel

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration** — every visual element should carry information or aid navigation. Ornament only when it reinforces the data.
2. **The green earns its place** — electric green (#22c55e) signals wins, active state, and primary actions. It is not a background or a fill; it is a signal.
3. **Dark is the native habitat** — dark mode is the default and must be treated as the primary surface. Light mode is a guest.
4. **Data is the hero** — typography hierarchy and spacing should make stats scannable at arm's length. Numbers should read before labels do.
5. **Consistent tension** — the design should feel like it has a slight edge: tight tracking, strong weight contrast, clipped corners, no soft/rounded-everywhere drift.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast on all surfaces (dark and light). Interactive focus states must be visible (currently using green glow rings — keep and extend). Reduced motion must suppress transforms and transitions.
